# Exercise 2: Short Reading

## Purpose
Quick reading comprehension. A short passage with 2 focused questions. Tests the ability to extract key information quickly — closer to real-world reading (emails, notices, short reports).

## Content

### The Passage
- 150-250 words
- Can be a different topic from the long reading, OR a related subtopic
- Slightly more accessible than the long reading (B1-B2 level)
- Could be styled as: a news brief, a short report, a letter excerpt, an informational blurb
- MUST use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences each) with clear line breaks between them — never one giant wall of text

### The Questions
- 2 questions total
- Types (pick 2, vary day to day):
  - **Multiple choice** (4 options)
  - **True / False / Not Given**
  - **Short answer** (1-3 words, typed by user) — e.g., "What year was the treaty signed?"
- Questions should require actually reading the passage (not guessable from the question alone)

## UI/UX

1. Passage displayed at top
2. 2 questions below
3. Appropriate input for each question type (radio buttons or short text field)
4. Single "Submit" button
5. Both must be answered before submit is enabled

## Feedback

After submission:
- Each question: user's answer, correct answer, 1-sentence explanation
- Green/red highlighting
- Score: "X/2"

## Scoring
- 2 points maximum
- Score stored as integer (0-2)
